Finding 03 · the business plan

How we actually run it.

1 · The Golden Rule (the compliance spine)

FREE = NO CONSIDERATION

Always a free way in

Register free online or grab a free clue-candy at the store. Nobody pays to enter or to improve their odds.

SKILL = NO CHANCE

Skill decides the winner

Puzzles, checkpoints, judge verification. No luck in who wins. The chest reveal is ceremony.

REAL PRIZE = NO DECEPTION

The treasure is real & proven

Documented, third-party-sealed, with a published end date and a stated outcome if it’s unfound.

🔐 The one rule under all the others: keep the money that funds the prize (sponsors) completely separate from the money participants pay (real BBQ, concert tickets, merch). A participant’s dollar may never buy odds, entry, or prize growth.

2 · The money model — three streams, zero gambling

STREAM A · PRIZE

Sponsor-funded & rising

Car-for-a-year, RV + season stay, a dream wedding, cash from $10k up. Grows on a published schedule as sponsors join — the media drumbeat.

STREAM B · OPERATIONS

Experiences people gladly buy

$100 BBQ event pass (real meal + access), $50 “In the Woods” concert tickets, merch. Funds the running of the event — never the prize.

STREAM C · LOCAL ENGINE

Shop-local “Blue Seal”

Spend at a participating merchant and 10% grows the prize amount (never odds or entry). The town co-owns the pot. See the Blue Seal below.

The Blue Seal — where a movement starts

The hunt isn’t just an event — it’s the incubator for the Blue Seal, a shop-local movement for Social Impact Capitalism. This is where it’s born.

HOW IT WORKS

10% grows the jackpot

Buy anything from a participating merchant and 10% of the price goes toward the jackpot. Spend $100 → the prize grows $10. You’re shopping local and pumping up the treasure.

YOUR NUMBER

One registration number

Register free and you get a number. Hand it to any merchant at checkout and your spend is recorded toward the jackpot. It tracks the pot — it never buys you better odds.

THE MOVEMENT

Every merchant becomes a member

Sign a merchant up and they become a founding Blue Seal member. The hunt seeds a town-wide shop-local network that outlives the event — a real Social Impact Capitalism movement.

⚖️ The compliance line (sign this off first): this is the one mechanic closest to “spend-and-win,” so the rails matter most. Registration and winning stay free and skill-based; spending only grows the prize amount, never your odds or eligibility. Keep that true and it’s a marketing/sponsorship pool, not gambling — but it is the first thing to run past AGLC and a lawyer.

4 · The trust & drumbeat engine

TRUST

Bi-weekly attestation

A community accountant or judge visits the hidden treasure every two weeks and signs a certificate it’s still there. Kills the “is it even real?” doubt — and each visit is its own news beat.

FRIDAYS · CFCW

Radio spin-the-wheel

Country radio spins a wheel 8am–noon that bumps the prize $100–$1,000 and drops one clue early on-air. At noon we announce the new total everywhere. Tune in to hear it first.

MIDWEEK

Double-Take days

Mon–Thu, when the park is quiet, the prize doubles if found that day — an excuse for midweek visitors to come out and hunt when no one else is around.

🍬 The merchant clue trail: every participating shop — Wabamun, the golf courses, mini-golf, the general store, the senior centre, the fish shop, Knickknacks on Ninth, even the town office — hands out free penny-candy with a clue in the wrapper (or its own posted clue). Gathering clues is skill and effort, not chance, and nothing is bought — so it’s clean, and it drives foot traffic to every business in the region.

Finding 03½ · the number

How big should the main prize be?

Set aside everything sponsors pile on. What should the headline cash prize be — and what does each level actually mean in a rural Alberta community an hour west of Edmonton?

RANK #4 · $10,000

The floor of the floor

Real money, but routine — Edmonton radio gives away $10k on a slow week. Reads as “nice,” not “drop everything.” Local press only.

Meaningful · mild
RANK #3 · $15,000

The smart announced floor

Credible and respectable — a used car, a serious debt dent. Already worth a multi-week hunt; a strong number to open on and grow from.

Meaningful · solid
RANK #2 · $20,000

The meaningfulness sweet spot

“Twenty grand” is life-changing for a working family — a down-payment dent, a year of college. This is where regional press leans in.

Meaningful · high
RANK #1 · $25,000

The headline ceiling

“Alberta’s biggest small-town treasure hunt.” Regional / provincial press potential. Best as the sponsor-funded crescendo target.

Meaningful · max
Recommendation

Announce a $15,000 floor — let it climb to $25,000 in public.

Because the prize is sponsor-funded and rising, the cost isn’t ours — the constraint is fundraising, not budget. So aim high. Open on a number that’s already credible ($15,000), make the climb the story (Friday radio spins, Double-Take days, new sponsors), and crescendo into the “$25,000 — biggest treasure hunt in Alberta” headline. If you must pick one fixed number, $20,000 is the meaningfulness sweet spot for this community.

Secondary prizes — and the “fork in the road”

Beyond the main cash, the hunt can hide bigger treasures and hand out smaller ones.

THE FORK

Find the dream wedding?

A 3-day wedding event can be found in the hunt. The design choice: does claiming it end your main-prize chase, or can you win both? My read: run them as separate parallel hunts so people can win both — more winners, more goodwill, simpler rules. Keep the “take-it-and-you’re-out” fork only if you want the extra drama, and disclose it loudly.

THE GIVEAWAYS

Prizes you don’t have to find

Hand out a flood of smaller prizes on-air at the radio station and around the park: a 2-night geodome stay, ice-fishing-hut outfitting, mini-golf and range passes, restaurant gift cards. Every one markets a park activity and keeps non-finders winning too.

Chaser Mode — the Catcher

Borrowed from Red Bull’s Wings for Life World Run: there is no fixed finish line — a Catcher Car starts behind you and speeds up until it passes you. People go dramatically farther fleeing a moving line than chasing a fixed one. So we turn the hunt into a chase.

THE CATCHER

A pursuer with a face

A virtual Catcher — give it a character and a voice (the Sasquatch, the Phantom Prospector) — advances across the map on a schedule. Stay ahead and you’re still in the running; let it pass your progress and your multiplier drops.

YOUR OWN RACE

Everyone is chased

Each player gets a Catcher tuned to their pace — a first-timer and an expert both feel the heat. The masses stay hooked, not just the one eventual winner.

THE DAILY SWEEP

Beat the morning sweep

Each day the Catcher sweeps the zone starting early — a reason to show up first thing, not roll in at noon.

OUTRUN = GIVE

Chase for charity

Wings for Life is a charity run. We do the same: every clue you solve ahead of the Catcher unlocks a sponsor donation to a local cause. Outrun the Catcher, fund the village — Social Impact Capitalism baked into the game itself.

🏃 Why it works: a fixed finish line lets you stop the moment you hit “good enough.” A Catcher behind you never lets you coast — loss aversion plus the thrill of pursuit pull people far past where a prize alone would. Your own Culture Chaser / Catcher Car already runs on this; the hunt is its outdoor cousin.

Draft Rules — v0.1

For Legal Review

The first honest draft

  • Winning is skill. Solve the clues, find the marker, and a judge verifies. No luck decides the winner.
  • Entry is free. Register free online or in person, and gather free candy-clues at merchants. You never pay to play or to improve your odds.
  • Stay on the board. Search only the in-bounds areas the app marks. Occupied RV sites, private homes, guest spaces, staff areas and the water’s edge are off-limits.
  • Respect guests. Do not enter or disturb anyone’s site or rental. Trespass or harassment means disqualification.
  • Be safe. No digging in unsafe spots, no after-dark searching where posted, children supervised. Safety within the rules is your responsibility.
  • A prize at every property. At least one prize is hidden at every property on the board — so it’s always worth searching every stop.
  • The prize is real. It’s hidden, documented, and attested every two weeks by a community accountant or judge.
  • The prize grows. Friday radio spins ($100–$1,000), Double-Take midweek days, and sponsor adds can raise the total — all announced publicly.
  • Secondary prizes have their own rules (see Prize Value). Radio-station giveaways are separate from the hunt.
  • Play fair. Family-friendly conduct; don’t publicly spoil clues you were given privately.
  • The fine print. Eligibility, the end date, the unfound-treasure outcome, prize taxes, and any skill-testing requirement live in the official rules.
⚠ Draft v0.1 — a starting point for the team and the lawyer, not final rules. The out-of-bounds zones and guest-privacy lines especially need a walk-through on the real property.
Finding 08 · we’re not the first

Five who proved it works — and what each teaches us.

2024 · USA

Project Skydrop

A shrinking-circle satellite hunt with a ~$130k prize, found in ~10 days, with a huge live online following. Teaches us: the shrinking circle + live countdown is gripping — but it mixed luck in. We keep the drama, swap luck for skill, and let sponsors fund the pot.

2010–2020 · Rockies

Forrest Fenn’s Treasure

A real ~$1–2M chest hidden with a poem of clues drew an estimated 350,000+ searchers and years of tourism across NM, WY and CO. Teaches us: a real treasure + a great story drives years of travel — and that wilderness risk is deadly serious, which is exactly why we stay on-property and skill-based.

1979 · UK

Masquerade (the golden hare)

Kit Williams’ picture book of clues to a real buried golden hare sold ~2M+ copies and launched a genre. Teaches us: the artifact + the narrative can become a product and a media franchise, not just an event.

2000–today · Global

Geocaching

3M+ hidden caches, millions of players, a proven driver of tourism and local-business foot traffic. Teaches us: a repeatable, community-owned “go-find-it” format reliably moves people to specific places — our properties.

1993–2024 · France

On the Trail of the Golden Owl

The longest-running armchair treasure hunt — a buried owl finally found after 31 years, sustaining a devoted community for three decades. Teaches us: mystery and longevity compound; an annual hunt becomes a brand people organize their lives around.

SMB · everywhere

Shop-local scavenger hunts & McMenamins Passport

Chambers, BIAs and multi-venue businesses use hunts/passports to drive foot traffic and build an owned audience. Teaches us: the lead-gen + shop-local model is already proven at exactly our scale — we’re just doing it bigger and better.

  • The real deliverable (an owned email list + traffic to five properties) is durable and compounds annually.
  • It costs little if sponsors carry the prize; downside is mostly time, not cash.

The five hard questions

  • Will sponsors actually sign? The whole model leans on it. If the car/RV/cash don’t materialize, there’s no rising-prize story. Get 2–3 signed before announcing anything.
  • Can we guarantee pure skill? If any luck taints “who wins” while money flows, AGLC can call it a lottery and shut it down. Legal sign-off is a hard gate, not a nicety.
  • Who runs it? Multi-week ops — clues, judging, social, sponsors, the event day — is real work. Name the owner and the hours before committing.
  • What if no one finds it? Real prize, disclosed rules, hard end date, graceful unfound outcome — or it reads as a phantom-prize scam.
  • Is the list worth the effort? Be honest about the number we expect to capture and what each contact is worth across the properties.

The risks worth naming

Regulatory (the killer — gate on legal). Sponsor dependency (no sponsors, no story). Execution load (under-staffed multi-week events fizzle). Weather/turnout (outdoor, seasonal). Brand (a flop or a compliance stumble dents “force for good”).

Verdict — conditional GO

Proceed to a Year-1 pilot, scoped small and clean: one hunt, fully sponsor-funded prize, free skill-based entry, the list as the real KPI. Gate the public launch on three things being true: (1) written legal/AGLC comfort on the pure-skill, free-entry, sponsor-funded design; (2) at least two anchor sponsors signed; (3) a named operator with the hours blocked. Hit those, and the upside dwarfs the cost. Miss any, and we wait.
